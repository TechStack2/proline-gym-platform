# PRAXELLA Design Audit — July 2026 (Phase 1: find everything, change nothing)

**Branch:** `prompt-design-audit` (report-only — zero product-code changes).
**Method:** full local stack (from-zero migration replay, cloud-parity grants), two seeded tenants — the teal demo gym (`demo`, `#0E7490`, brand-follow visible) and a NULL-brand gym (`nullbrand`, crimson defaults) — swept by the capture harness at `scripts/design-audit/capture.mjs` across **38 surfaces × viewports 360/390/768/1280/1920 × en/ar × light/dark** (≈400 page-loads, 500+ screenshots, 0 capture failures on the final pass), plus a code-level PWA-chrome audit and a full component-inconsistency inventory of `src/`.
**Evidence:** curated screenshots in [`shots/`](shots/) (referenced per finding); the complete raw pack + per-load metrics (`metrics.jsonl`: horizontal overflow, sub-44px tap targets, console errors, applied theme/dir) lives in the untracked `design-audit-artifacts/` + `design-audit-rerun/` dirs on the audit machine. Code findings cite `file:line` on this branch.

**Grading.** Severity: **A** = broken/embarrassing in front of a paying gym owner · **B** = inconsistent/unpolished · **C** = opportunity. Effort: **S** (hours) / **M** (days) / **L** (a slice+). Category: layout-spacing · component-inconsistency · navigation-ergonomics · color-token · type-rtl · dark-mode · native-feel · a11y · i18n-copy.

**Overall verdict.** The bones are good: zero horizontal body overflow on **every** page/viewport/locale (measured), disciplined logical-property RTL (0 unguarded `ml-/mr-`, all chevrons flipped), a real channel-var token system with byte-identical light mode, per-role shell identity, and a genuinely strong staff desktop. The debt is concentrated in six places: raw-hex escapes from the token system (all the dark-mode breakage), bottom-tab IA past capacity (portal 7 / coach 6), a portal/coach desktop chrome that was never designed, absent bidi isolation for LTR data in Arabic, an install/PWA chrome layer that is authored but inert or mis-tenanted, and partial adoption of the ui primitives (231 raw `<button>` vs 141 `<Button>`).

## Severity A — broken / embarrassing

**DA-1 · dark-mode · S — Every mobile page title is invisible in dark mode, in every shell.**
`NativeHeader` hardcodes `text-[#252525]` for the collapsed, large and compact titles ([NativeHeader.tsx:154](../../src/components/native/NativeHeader.tsx#L154), [:205](../../src/components/native/NativeHeader.tsx#L205), [:217](../../src/components/native/NativeHeader.tsx#L217)) and `SwipeableSheet` for its sheet title ([SwipeableSheet.tsx:266](../../src/components/native/SwipeableSheet.tsx#L266)). Raw hexes don't flip with the channel-var inversion, so "Today", "Home", "Schedule", "الجدول"… render near-black-on-near-black on **every** mobile page across staff, portal and coach in both locales. Desktop titles are token-backed and fine. Shots: `shots/staff/today--390-en-dark.png`, `shots/portal/home--390-en-dark.png`, `shots/coach/today--390-en-dark.png`, `shots/staff/inbox--390-en-dark.png`.

**DA-2 · native-feel · S — Safe-area padding is authored everywhere but inert on iOS: no `viewport-fit=cover` anywhere.**
The shells dutifully pad with `env(safe-area-inset-*)` (NativeTabBar.tsx:71,168; NativeHeader.tsx:122; DashboardLayoutClient.tsx:151; PortalLayoutClient.tsx:80; MoreMenuSheet.tsx:35) — but no layout's `viewport`/`generateViewport` export sets `viewportFit: 'cover'` (the four exports set only `themeColor`), so on notched iPhones in standalone mode every `env()` resolves to 0: the tab bar sits behind the home indicator and the header under the status bar. One-line fix per shell; code-proven (not screenshot-able in Chromium).

**DA-3 · navigation-ergonomics · M — The portal (7-tab) and coach (6-tab) bottom bars are past capacity: labels truncate in Arabic at 390 — the primary locale.**
`PORTAL_TABS` ships seven primary tabs and `COACH_TABS` six, with no "More" overflow (unlike staff's 4+More) — [PortalTabConfig.ts](../../src/app/[locale]/portal/_components/PortalTabConfig.ts), [CoachTabConfig.ts](../../src/app/[locale]/coach/_components/CoachTabConfig.ts). At 390/ar the portal ellipsizes "الملف الـ…" (Profile) and "تدريب خا…" (PT) and coach clips "الملف الشخ…"; at 360/en "Schedu…"/"Attendan…" truncate; targets shrink to ~52px with ~10px labels. iOS convention caps at 5. This is the owner-named footer-menu finding and needs an IA decision, not CSS. Shots: `shots/portal/home--390-ar-light.png`, `shots/portal/home--360-en-light.png`, `shots/coach/today--360-en-light.png`.

**DA-4 · layout-spacing · M — Portal + coach desktop chrome is broken: the header renders under the fixed rail on every ≥768 page.**
`PortalLayoutClient`/`CoachLayoutClient` give only `<main>` the rail clearance (`md:ms-20`, and in ar `md:me-20` — the wrong logical side under RTL); the full-width `NativeHeader` gets none, so the MEMBER/COACH badge slides under the rail — clipped orange/graphite fragments visible top-start on **every** desktop capture of both shells (LTR and RTL), plus an unpainted corner seam above the rail. [PortalLayoutClient.tsx:66](../../src/app/[locale]/portal/_components/PortalLayoutClient.tsx#L66), [CoachLayoutClient.tsx:65](../../src/app/[locale]/coach/_components/CoachLayoutClient.tsx#L65). **DA-16 · native-feel · M — The installed-app (PWA identity) layer is half-built: locale-less start, hardcoded lang/dir, foreign splash, no Apple icons.**

No `apple-touch-icon` link and no `appleWebApp` metadata exist anywhere → iOS home-screen installs get degraded icons/status-bar behavior. `themeColor` tracks OS `prefers-color-scheme`, not the app's own `html.dark` toggle, so an OS-light/app-dark user gets a mismatched status bar. Legacy `public/manifest.json` ("PRO LINE Gym", `theme_color #cd1419`) still ships beside the dynamic manifest. Code-proven cluster; one slice fixes all.

## Synthesis — the systemic root causes (Phase-2 seeds)

1. **Raw-hex escape hatches in core chrome.** The token system is real and works (light byte-identical, dark inverts, brand ramps) — every dark-mode A/B finding traces to values that bypassed it: `text-[#252525]` titles, pastel tint cards, role-color hex maps, native inputs, light-pinned chips. Phase 2 rule: *no color literal outside the token files; designed-fixed surfaces get explicit fixed tokens* (the DS-3 rule, generalized).
2. **No enforced primitive layer.** button/card/badge/input/select primitives exist but adoption is 30–60%, `ui/select` is used once, and there is no PageHeader, no Dialog, no EmptyState, no StatusChip. Every consistency finding (four tab styles, duplicated status pills, focus-ring drift, three empty-state tiers) is a missing-or-unadopted primitive.
3. **Tab-bar IA grew by accretion past its capacity.** Staff got the 4+More design; portal (7) and coach (6) just kept appending. There is no capacity rule, no overflow pattern outside staff, and the bar's ARIA is a tablist pretending. One shared, capacity-capped, properly-labeled TabBar with a role-specific IA decision is the fix — CSS cannot save seven tabs at 390.
4. **No shared formatting/bidi layer.** Dates in three formats, mixed clocks, currency symbol sides, phone `+` flips, raw enums, raw i18n keys, `toLocaleString` dumps — every value that meets Arabic text or a user's eye is formatted ad hoc. Phase 2: one `fmt` module (date/time/money/phone/enum-label) with mandatory bidi isolation, plus a missing-key CI gate.
5. **The PWA chrome layer is authored but not engaged.** Safe-area padding exists with no `viewport-fit=cover`; the per-tenant manifest exists but hardcodes start_url/lang/dir/splash/description; Apple install metadata is absent; the install promo speaks the wrong tenant, role, device and language. "Installable" shipped; "installed-grade" did not.
6. **Two desktop strategies.** Staff desktop was designed (sidebar, search header, real grids); portal/coach desktop is the mobile shell with a rail — header under the rail, phone column in a void, wrong-side RTL margin. Phase 2 must decide: either portal/coach desktop is a first-class layout (recommended: they're the customer-facing shells) or it's explicitly a centered mobile frame — the half-state is the worst of both.
7. **No zero/empty-state doctrine.** Alarm-colored $0s with live CTAs, "Inbox zero" above pending work, marketing copy interpolating 0, placeholder walls on public pages, dead zero-count filters — versus the exemplary Notifications empty state. One doctrine: zeros are calm, summaries must agree with their page, public surfaces collapse rather than placeholder.
8. **Brand and semantic color are one ramp.** Crimson is simultaneously brand, action, error, category and logout; the landing doesn't consume the tenant ramp it emits; portal/coach chrome shows no tenant identity. Phase 2: split roles (brand / action / destructive / status / category-neutral) and finish the WL story on the landing + install layer.
